Objective

I’m interested in developing my knowledge of comparative and international policy. As a One Young World Ambassador, and one of five Canadian delegates to the Youth 7 Summit in Tokyo in 2016, I have grown my skills in diplomacy and representation. In 2014, I studied abroad at Sciences Po Paris, Campus Euro-Americain de Reims and is fluently bilingual in French and English.

I am excited to graduate in June 2017 from a Joint Honours Bachelor of Social Sciences in Communication and Political Science, with Co-op from the University of Ottawa. Having participated in four fulfilling Co-operative Education terms, held part-time work during academic semesters, and a variety of career-track volunteer roles; I am excited to move forward in a rewarding career.

What I Do ?

With an eye for opportunities for innovation, I seize the chance to connect Canadians and stakeholders with their governments. Blending civil service with civil society, I work to integrate new technology with traditional governance.

With research and academic interests in digital government, citizen engagement, civic education, and Canadian federalism, I’m passionate about creating evidence-based policy and sharing solutions.
